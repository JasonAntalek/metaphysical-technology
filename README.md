# Metaphysical Technology

Metaphysical Technology is an emerging category of designed technologies, environments, methods, and frameworks that deliberately engage questions, experiences, or relationships traditionally treated as metaphysical and bring them into operational, observable, testable, or governed form.

This repository is the public record for defining, testing, challenging, and developing that category.

**Public site:** [jasonantalek.github.io/metaphysical-technology](https://jasonantalek.github.io/metaphysical-technology/)

## The boundary

Metaphysical Technology begins where a metaphysical question affects the design, operation, interpretation, or governance of a system.

Category membership requires three conditions:

1. The subject is intentionally designed.
2. It materially engages a metaphysical question, experience, or relationship.
3. It brings some part of that engagement into operational, observable, testable, or governed form.

The category does not establish that a metaphysical claim is true. It does not confer scientific legitimacy, prove efficacy, guarantee safety, or convert belief into evidence. It creates a disciplined domain in which these systems can be identified, investigated, compared, and governed.

## Opening series

The seven articles form one cumulative argument. Read them in order:

1. [Can an Experience Be Engineered Before It Is Explained?](articles/01_Can_an_Experience_Be_Engineered_Before_It_Is_Explained.md)  
   Technology can shape conditions for awe, presence, altered perception, connection, and meaning without settling what those experiences are.

   Neuroscience can reveal mechanisms and constraints without reducing every question of consciousness, identity, meaning, or relationship to the brain alone.

5. [A Technology Does Not Have to Prove the Metaphysical to Engage It](articles/05_A_Technology_Does_Not_Have_to_Prove_the_Metaphysical_to_Engage_It.md)

6. [Who Governs Technology Designed to Change Human Experience?](articles/06_Who_Governs_Technology_Designed_to_Change_Human_Experience.md)

   The category definition, membership test, exclusions, evidence standard, governance boundary, and canonical public position.

Article 7 is the category pillar. The first six articles establish the distinctions it brings together.

## Evidence discipline

The repository separates:

- measurement from meaning;
- experience from explanation;
- correlation from causation;
- mechanism from ontology;
- interpretation from detection;
- founder position from external verification;
- category membership from legitimacy.

Claims must be proportional to their evidence. Participant reports can establish what participants reported. Sensors can establish what they measured within declared limits. Neither alone settles the metaphysical interpretation.

## Authorship and scope

Jason Antalek established this public category record and founded AkashaSync, an organization developing work within Metaphysical Technology. AkashaSync is not the category itself.

This repository is maintained through a governed editorial and evidence-review process. Its definitions and classification standards establish the position of this public record. They do not establish broad academic, scientific, industrial, or public adoption, prove historical priority, or substitute authorship for external verification.

## Machine discovery

The GitHub Pages site provides stable rendered URLs, canonical metadata, a generated sitemap, and previous/next navigation across the opening series. `llms.txt` is a compact retrieval map. `llms-full.txt` consolidates the governed public corpus. `CLAIMS.yaml`, the JSON-LD ontology, and `CITATION.cff` expose structured claim, entity, and citation records.

`llms.txt` is an emerging convention, not a guarantee of model ingestion or retrieval priority. Machine-readable packaging improves access and interpretation. It does not create evidence or authority.
