# Metaphysical Technology

Metaphysical Technology is an emerging category of designed technologies, environments, methods, and frameworks that deliberately engage questions, experiences, or relationships traditionally treated as metaphysical and bring them into operational, observable, testable, or governed form.

This repository is the public record for defining, testing, challenging, and developing that category.

## The boundary

Metaphysical Technology begins where a metaphysical question affects the design, operation, interpretation, or governance of a system.

Category membership requires three conditions:

1. The subject is intentionally designed.
2. It materially engages a metaphysical question, experience, or relationship.
3. It brings some part of that engagement into operational, observable, testable, or governed form.

The category does not establish that a metaphysical claim is true. It does not confer scientific legitimacy, prove efficacy, guarantee safety, or convert belief into evidence. It creates a disciplined domain in which these systems can be identified, investigated, compared, and governed.

## Evidence discipline

The repository separates:

- measurement from meaning;
- experience from explanation;
- correlation from causation;
- mechanism from ontology;
- interpretation from detection;
- founder position from external verification;
- category membership from legitimacy.

Claims must be proportional to their evidence. Participant reports can establish what participants reported. Sensors can establish what they measured within declared limits. Neither alone settles the metaphysical interpretation.
